Speech Emotion Recognition Incorporating Relative Difficulty and Labeling Reliability

Guess emotional state for the utterance-level speech


Youngdo Ahn1, Sangwook Han1, Seonggyu Lee1, Jeom Ja Kang2, and Jong Won Shin1

1Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology, Korea, 2Electronics and Telecommunications Research Institute, Korea


Most speech emotion recognition studies aim to classify emotion labels on audio-visual datasets.
This experiment shows the difficulty of speech emotion recognition with datasets where humans annotated for audio-visual data.
This page is for research demonstration purposes only.

CREMA-D examples

Each example is annotated by around 30 evaluators.
Evaluators watched the utterance-level video and labeled among "anger (A), disgust (D), fear (F), happy (H), neutral (N), sad (S)".
True label stands for the majority vote result of the evaluations.
Please guess the emotion label after listening to "Speech only" and watching "Video" respectively, and then compare your labels with the "True label".
